SIR ISAAC NEWTON

Here's a time line of Isaac Newton's life:

  • 1642 - Born in Woolsthorpe
  • 1653 - Sent to grammar school in Grantham, England, to prepare for university
  • 1661 - Newton begins his undergraduate studies at Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1664 - Newton writes the "Certain Philosophical Questions"
    This contains the popular slogan "Aristotle is my friend, Plato is my friend, but my best friend is truth"
  • 1665 - Recieves his bachelor's degree
  • 1667 - elected to a fellowship at Trinity College after the university reopens after the plague
  • 1668 - Newton invents the reflecting telescope
  • 1669 - Newton becomes a professor of mathematics at Trinity College.
    He writes "On Analysis by Equations with an Inifinite Number of Terms
  • 1672 - Newton sends a paper on light and colors to the Royal Society.
  • 1684 - Newton sends a short tract entitled "On Motion" to British astronomer Edmond Hallley
  • 1696 - Newton is appointed warden of the mint
  • 1703 - Newton is elected president of the Royal Society
  • 1704 - Newton publishes the first edition of optics
  • 1705 - Queen Anne knights Newton, the first time a scientist receives the honor
  • 1727 - Newton dies in London, England.
